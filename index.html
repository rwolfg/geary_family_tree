What this tells us

Your DNA is a mosaic of medieval European migrations. The largest single bloc — 27% Southern Germanic — confirms the Eierman/Bohemian-Czech cluster as your most genetically dominant line. The 21% Central Scotland/Northern Ireland is the single biggest surprise: you are more Scots-Irish than any single documented line suggests, pointing to untraced ancestry in those regions.

The 9% Slovene/Balkan signal almost certainly traces to Joseph Rousek and Mary Hurka — Bohemia borders Austria and Slovenia, and migration between these regions was common. The 8% Sweden, 2% Denmark, 2% Norway together total 12% Scandinavian — far more than any named ancestor can explain.

The 1% Western Bantu and 1% Madeira are the most intriguing mysteries: small percentages in ancestry tests can indicate a single ancestor 5–8 generations back, or can reflect ancient population movements — but at 1% each, they deserve investigation.
